You have being assigned to the role of Player B and you are part of a group of 3 participants (including yourself). The 3 members of your group are all Players B.

All members of your group participated in Part 1 and earned an income from it.

You are now asked to report the income you earned in Part 1 starting from the {{ Constants.payoff_trial }} you earned in the practice round, to calculate your final earnings. The income that you report will be taxed at the rate of {{Constants.tax_rate}}%.

The other Players B in your group are also asked to report their incomes for taxation. Your income report, as well as the income report of the other players in your group, is completely private. You won't know their income report, and they won't know yours.

Taxes collected will be doubled and given to the organisation Keep Britain Tidy at the end of this study. The money will be used to support its actions of reducing litter, improving local places, and preventing waste in the UK.

Your group is going to be randomly matched with 1 Player A out of 10 Players A. In a previous study, the 10 Players A already decided whether to take {{Constants.money_to_take}} from the collected taxes of the group of Players B they are going to be matched with.

They could take the {{Constants.money_to_take}} from the collected taxes before being doubled and transferred to Keep Britain Tidy. These {{Constants.money_to_take}} points correspond to the collected taxes coming from the group of Players B's earnings in the practice round in Part 1.
